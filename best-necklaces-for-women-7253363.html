The 22 Best Necklaces for Women of 2024

A necklace is a simple way to add some personality to your everyday look. If you wear a lot of basics, you might consider a more unique necklace to create some intrigue through texture (like a chain) or a focal point (like a pendant). Alternatively, you can layer necklaces and combine different styles to assemble the perfect neck dressing.

“If you are looking to simply grow your everyday collection, a necklace designed for layering is a great choice,” celebrity jeweler Kendra Scott tells PEOPLE. “These are often long, thin, and delicate necklaces, and may feature smaller gemstones or pendants.” She suggests finding necklaces of different lengths so you can layer them in fun arrangements.

Regardless of what style, metal, or length you choose, a necklace can add a level of whimsy or sophistication to your look. Read on to see our picks for the best necklaces for women (or anyone who wants a bit of bling).

How to Pick the Right Necklace

Material

The material of a necklace can determine how it looks and how long it lasts. “The metal material used is the most important way to determine the quality and wearability of the piece,” says Scott. She explains that standard “fashion jewelry” is usually 14k gold-plated, which maintains the look of gold but at a more budget-friendly price, like the gold-plated BaubleBar Dulce 18K Gold Necklace. Whereas, real gold will last longer, but costs more, such as the MadBeret Roman Letter Charm in solid 14k gold. “Invest in the pieces you wear daily — the 14K yellow, white, or rose gold you choose as your core items will last a lifetime,” she says. 

Length

The length of your necklace will determine where it sits across your neck line. “The more length options you have with a piece, the more versatile it is,” says Scott. She adds that you can use the “extender chain on a necklace” to adjust the length, or buy several lengths to create a layered look. For instance, it can spark visual interest to pair a collar-length one and a longer necklace, such as the Mejuri Floating Sapphire Necklace and the Made by Mary Skinny Vertical Bar Necklace.

Style

There are so many styles of necklaces, it’s almost impossible to choose just one (and you don’t have to!). For those who want to keep it simple, a thin chain or short collar necklace adds some glimmer to your look without overpowering anything. On the other hand, if you want a bolder necklace, you can rock a statement piece like the Ashton Gold Half Chain Necklace in White Pearl. “If shopping for a special occasion, styling your look with a statement piece is a great way to elevate your look and show off your personal style,” says Scott.

She adds that you can personalize a necklace as a custom style. “You can always add charms, or engrave a meaningful message, number or letter to help tell your own story through jewelry,” Scott says. 

Price

The last element of necklace shopping to keep in mind is price, but it’s an important one. Necklaces can range the gamut in cost since various materials and gems have different values. Plated pieces will offer the look of real gold or silver, but sell at a lower cost — like the Ellena Rose Sterling Silver Opal Necklace, which comes in at $25 — whereas real gold types, and especially those with gems, will be quite a bit pricier. And if diamonds are involved, you should have your checkbook ready, of course.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do certain metals last longer than others?

    Yes, they do. “Due to the materials used, fine and demi-fine jewelry will last longer than fashion jewelry,” Scott explains. “However, you can keep a fashion piece for many years if you care for it properly.” She says you should always remove plated jewelry when you’re dealing with water — washing hands, swimming, showering, exercising — or cleaning, applying lotion, or dealing with other ingredients or chemicals. Most jewelry will come with a pouch of some sort, and these are great to store your necklace in, as well as using a polishing cloth “to keep it looking bright and shiny,” Scott adds. However, if you’re talking about fine jewelry, you can wear it the majority of the time without concern.

  • What necklace length should I choose?

    Scott says there’s no real method to finding the right length, it just depends on preference. “Your jewelry box should be filled with a variety of sizes — both in length and scale to create a textural and eclectic feel,” she says. Scott suggests having a mix of chain and pendant necklaces to mix and match on different occasions.

  • Are thicker or thinner chains better?

    Scott says choosing between thick and thin chains “is like asking [her] to pick a favorite child.” In short, both styles can be versatile additions to your jewelry box. She says thicker chains are trending for their versatility right now because they’re “not as bold as a statement necklace” but can be worn on their own. “The thicker chain also adds variety to your everyday jewelry,” Scott adds. “You can style it with your everyday pieces for a new and fresh look.”
